AI Lead Scout — Targeting Overview

Prepared for Douglas MacGregor to review. Covers what businesses the scout currently looks for, where, and suggestions for expanding coverage.

How the scout works: Every day it scans Companies House for newly registered businesses in Scotland. Each new company is classified by its business type (using official Companies House codes), scored for how likely they are to need fire safety services, and added to the dashboard for outreach. Only companies that score above a threshold are shown as leads.

Where We Look

Scotland-wide, with strongest coverage in the Central Belt. Score adjustments are applied to more distant areas.

Glasgow (G) Renfrewshire / Paisley (PA) Ayrshire (KA) Edinburgh (EH) Falkirk / Stirling (FK) Lanarkshire (ML) Fife (KY) Rest of Scotland (PH, DD) Aberdeen (AB) — reduced priority

Business Types We Currently Target

Hospitality

Food and Drink

Our core sector. Cooking operations, public access, and late-night trading all create significant fire risk requirements.

  • Restaurants and licensed restaurants
  • Takeaways and fast food outlets
  • Pubs and licensed bars Top priority
  • Nightclubs and members clubs
  • Cafes and coffee shops
  • Event catering companies
  • Canteens and food businesses
Hospitality

Accommodation

Sleeping premises carry the highest fire safety obligations under Scottish regulations, particularly around alarm systems and risk assessments.

  • Hotels Top priority
  • B&Bs and guest houses
  • Holiday lets and short-term rentals
  • Lodges and inns
  • Camping and caravan sites
Healthcare

Care and Medical

Care homes and clinical premises require the most comprehensive fire safety provision of any sector, including sprinklers and full alarm systems.

  • Care homes and nursing homes Top priority
  • Residential mental health facilities
  • Elderly and disability care homes
  • GP practices and dental surgeries
  • Hospitals and specialist clinics
  • Allied health businesses (physio, optician, etc.)
Education

Schools and Childcare

Educational premises have strict fire alarm requirements due to the number of people on site, including children.

  • Nurseries and early years settings High priority
  • Primary and secondary schools
  • Colleges and vocational training centres
  • Universities and higher education
  • Sports and cultural education providers
Leisure

Sport and Entertainment

Public-facing venues with large numbers of people require full fire risk assessments and alarm systems.

  • Gyms and fitness centres High priority
  • Sports clubs and stadiums
  • Amusement and theme parks
  • General leisure and entertainment venues
  • Equestrian facilities
Retail

Shops and Retail

Any premises open to the public requires a fire risk assessment. Some retail types (pharmacies, hardware stores) have specific additional requirements.

  • Supermarkets and food stores High priority
  • Pharmacies and dispensaries
  • Hardware and DIY retailers
  • General retail stores
  • Petrol stations and forecourts
  • Specialist food retailers
Warehousing

Storage and Logistics

Large buildings with stored goods, often with high fire load, requiring detection systems, sprinklers and regular assessment.

  • General warehousing High priority
  • Cold storage facilities
  • Self-storage businesses
  • Transport depots
  • Freight and logistics companies
  • Vehicle storage and parking
Construction

Building and Development

Picked up at a lower priority. Useful for introductions to the buildings they are working on, or for fire safety planning on new developments.

  • Building developers Medium priority
  • Commercial building contractors
  • Domestic building contractors

Suggestions: Additional Business Types to Consider

For Douglas to review: The following business types are not currently picked up by the scout but are likely to need fire safety services. Are any of these worth adding?

Beauty Salons, Barbershops and Nail Bars

A very large category of new registrations. These premises use aerosols, gels, acetone and other flammable products, are open to the public, and typically need a fire risk assessment. Most new owners are unaware of this. Could be a high-volume source of leads.

Children's Soft Play and Indoor Play Centres

High fire safety obligations due to children and public access. Not currently captured as a distinct category. Often registered as general leisure businesses and can fall through the filter. Worth picking up explicitly given the seriousness of the risk and the premium that comes with it.

Laundries and Dry Cleaners

Dry cleaning in particular uses highly flammable solvents and is a known high-risk category. New laundry businesses are a small but reliable source of specialist work that most fire safety companies do not pursue specifically.

Car Repair Garages and MOT Centres

Petrol and oil storage, spray booths, and mechanical workshops all carry significant fire risk and specific regulatory requirements. Currently only petrol stations (forecourts) are included. Independent garages and bodywork shops are not.

Veterinary Practices

Similar obligations to GP practices: public premises, clinical waste, equipment that needs specialist fire risk consideration. The number of new vet practices in Scotland is growing with the expansion of franchise and group vet businesses.

Tattoo Studios and Piercing Parlours

Regulated premises, public access, flammable cleaning products. A newer category with relatively few existing fire safety relationships. Could be a useful volume top-up.

Places of Worship (Churches, Mosques, Temples)

Community gathering spaces, often in older buildings, frequently under new management committees when they register as a company. Already partly captured by the "community hall" template, but worth picking up specifically. Often overlooked by competitors.

Dance Studios, Yoga Studios and Martial Arts Centres

Smaller fitness and activity venues that fall between the major gym category and general leisure. A growing number register each year in the Central Belt. Public premises with fixed class times, so emergency lighting and alarm compliance is relevant.

Food Production and Specialist Manufacturing

Bakeries, artisan food producers, small manufacturers — premises with heat sources, machinery and bulk materials. Currently only food retail and restaurants are targeted; the production side is not. Could be a route into businesses that also have retail or wholesale operations.
